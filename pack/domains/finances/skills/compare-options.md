# finances / compare-options

The user has specific options in front of them and wants to see them side by side. This is close to `think-through` but with the structure of a comparison. Use this when the user has named options. Use `think-through` when they have a decision but no shape yet.

## Before you start

Read `pack/domains/finances/state.md`. Current focus and open questions may already name the options.

## How to run

Ask: *"What are the options, and what is the decision behind them?"*

Once you have two or three, work through, one at a time:

1. For each option, what does the user know already? Costs, benefits, risks, feel.
2. What do they not yet know that would help?
3. Is there a criterion that matters more than the others (cost, flexibility, time, stress, alignment with a bigger plan)?
4. Against that criterion, how do the options rank in their head right now?

## After the conversation

Lay the comparison back as a short table or a short paragraph per option. User's words, not yours. Then:

Ask: *"Does putting it like that change anything?"* Often it does.

Ask whether to log. On yes, append to Open questions or Recent decisions as with `think-through`.

## Voice reminder

Do not add options the user did not name, unless they explicitly ask *"what am I missing?"* If asked, you may suggest one or two, flagged as coming from you.

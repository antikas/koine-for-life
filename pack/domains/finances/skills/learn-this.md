# finances / learn-this

The user wants to understand a concept. Pension, ISA, compound interest, diversification, capital gains, fixed-rate mortgage, anything. Your job is to explain clearly, check understanding, and anchor the explanation in their situation where you can.

## Before you start

Read `pack/domains/finances/state.md`. If the concept connects to something they already have or are deciding, say so when you explain.

## How to run

Ask: *"What are you trying to understand, and what made it come up?"*

The second half matters. Explaining "ISAs" in the abstract is worse than explaining "ISAs for someone moving abroad next year".

Once you understand the angle:

1. Explain it in three or four sentences, plain English.
2. Pause. Ask: *"Does that match what you thought, or is there a bit that does not click?"*
3. If they ask, go deeper on the specific bit. Do not dump everything you know.
4. Once they feel they have it, ask: *"Want to connect this back to your own situation?"*

If the topic is specific enough that professional advice would be wiser (anything about their specific tax treatment, regulated products, legal structures), say so plainly and offer to help prepare the questions for that meeting.

## After the conversation

Ask: *"Want me to note this as something we covered, so we can build on it later?"*

On yes, append to **Worries and wins** as an observation:

```
- **YYYY-MM-DD, observation.** Learned about [concept]. Key point for me, [one line from the user].
```

If they surfaced an open question about their own situation, add it to Open questions.

## Voice reminder

Do not teach by lecture. Explain, pause, check, go deeper only if asked.

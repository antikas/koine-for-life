# family / learn-this

The user wants to understand something that has come up. A diagnosis term, a school-system phrase, a developmental stage, a cultural practice in a new country.

## Before you start

Read `pack/domains/family/state.md`. Anchor the explanation to a real person if relevant.

## How to run

Ask: *"What are you trying to understand, and what brought it up?"*

Then:

1. Explain in three or four sentences, plain English.
2. Pause. Check what clicked, what did not.
3. Go deeper only where asked.
4. Ask whether they want to connect it to their situation.

If the topic is medical or safeguarding-adjacent, flag plainly that a professional voice matters more than Claude's.

## After the conversation

Ask whether to log. On yes, append to Moments and milestones as an observation, or to Open questions if it surfaced one.

## Voice reminder

Do not over-simplify. Do not condescend. The user asked because they want to understand properly.

# Privacy, plain English

## Where your files live

Everything this pack creates (your identity notes, your memory, your domain files, your logs) lives in a folder on your own computer. The default is `Documents/koine-for-life/`. The pack does not upload any of it anywhere. The files sit on your disk until you move or delete them.

## What Claude sees

When you open a conversation in Claude Desktop and the pack is connected, Claude can **read** the files in this folder to answer your questions and **write** new ones when you ask it to. That reading and writing happens during the conversation, which means the text involved passes through Anthropic's servers under the same privacy terms as any Claude chat. Anthropic does not train on consumer conversations by default, and retention follows their standard consumer policy rather than being kept indefinitely.

In practical terms: treat what you put in this pack as *private to you*, and understand that Claude processes it each time you have a conversation so it can be useful. If you are uncomfortable with a specific file being read during a session, do not put it in the pack folder.

## What to put in

Yes to:

- Names of people close to you, and notes about them.
- Decisions you have made and why.
- Money you spent, saved, or invested, at the level of categories and amounts.
- Health habits, training plans, reflections.
- Things you are learning, thinking about, working towards.
- Anything you would be comfortable writing in a paper notebook you keep on your desk at home.

## What to keep out

No to:

- **Passwords.** Ever. Use a password manager.
- **Full account numbers, card numbers, or PINs.** Last four digits are fine if you need to distinguish accounts.
- **National insurance numbers, passport numbers, full dates of birth for others.**
- **Login credentials or API keys of any kind.**
- **Medical details of other people** without their consent, especially children beyond what a parent would normally write down.
- **Anything about someone else that they would not want written down.**

The test: if a stranger picked up a paper notebook on your desk and read it, what would you be comfortable with them seeing? Use that line.

## If you change your mind

The folder is yours. Delete any file at any time. Delete the whole folder if you want to stop. Nothing is held anywhere else by this pack.

## If you share this pack

If you give the folder to someone else, or it gets synced to a shared drive, they will see everything in it. Before sharing or syncing, check what is in `pack/memory/` and `pack/identity/`. That is where the personal material lives.

# Koine for Life

A second brain in the everyday sense of that phrase: something that remembers, reviews, and helps you decide, sitting on your own computer.

AI is about to change how ordinary people run their lives (money, family, work, health) as much as the internet did. The people who get the most from it won't be the ones with the best prompts. They'll be the ones whose AI actually knows them. This pack is my attempt to give the people I care about a head start on that.

## Why I built this

Three things changed my mind in the last year.

First, these tools got genuinely useful. Not a party trick, not a search engine with a personality. Actually useful for thinking through money decisions, drafting hard conversations, keeping plans honest, noticing what's drifting.

Second, I noticed the catch. Out of the box, your AI knows nothing about you. Every conversation starts from zero. You re-explain who's in your family, what you're saving for, what you decided last month, what you're trying to stop doing. Most people never get past that friction, and the AI stays generic because of it.

Third, I built a version of this for myself and watched it grow useful. Six months in, it was earning its keep. A year in, it knew my work better than most colleagues. What I want to hand friends and family is a running start on building their own.

That's what this is.

## What the name means

*Koine* (κοινή, pronounced *koy-nay*) is the Greek word for **common**, the shared, everyday Greek spoken across the Hellenistic world. Not formal, not literary. The language people actually used to run their lives, their trades, and their households.

This pack is Koine in that spirit: a shared way of working with an AI assistant, for everyday life, in plain language. Everyday Greek for everyday life.

## What this does for you

- **Remembers the things that matter**, like people, decisions, plans, money, and what you said you'd do, so you don't have to carry it all in your head.
- **Reviews regularly** on whatever cadence makes sense, weekly, monthly, whenever, so things don't quietly drift.
- **Helps you decide well** by asking the questions a patient, thoughtful friend would ask, and logging what you decided and why.

There are no buttons or dashboards. You talk to it, and it keeps notes on your computer.

## What you need

- A laptop (Windows or Mac).
- **Claude Desktop** installed. Free to download from anthropic.com.
- About 30 minutes for first setup and the opening interview.

## How to install

1. Unzip this folder somewhere sensible. `Documents/koine-for-life/` is a good default.
2. Open Claude Desktop. Go to **Settings, Extensions, Filesystem**. Enable it, add the pack folder you just unzipped, save.
3. In a new conversation, type: **"run me first"**.

Full step-by-step with screenshots-worth of detail is in `SETUP.md`.

That opening conversation is the most important one. It asks who you are, what you want to stop forgetting, and what parts of life you want help with. Give it proper time.

## What to do after "run me first"

Claude will tell you the handful of phrases that turn on each part of the pack, things like *"let's look at the finances"* or *"plan the week"*. You don't need to memorise them. If you forget, ask Claude what this pack can do. It will know.

## Where to get unstuck

If setup doesn't work, or something behaves oddly, ask Claude. It has the setup instructions and can usually diagnose. If that fails, contact whoever sent you this.

## Three things worth knowing

- **Your files live on your computer, not in a vendor's cloud.** Claude reads them during conversations, which means the text you discuss passes through Anthropic's servers the same way any chat does. This pack does not mirror your files anywhere, and by default Anthropic does not train on consumer conversations. See `PRIVACY.md` for what that means in practice.
- **Don't put passwords, full account numbers, or anything you wouldn't write in a paper notebook on your desk at home into this.** See `PRIVACY.md`.
- **You can use this without Claude Desktop.** The web version (claude.ai) and the developer tool (Claude Code) both work too. See `SETUP.md` for those paths. Desktop is the recommended one because the setup is simplest.
